🚀 Ascent

Functional Design Document

Purpose: User experience specification for LeaderReps Ascent Audience: Product stakeholders, leadership team Created: March 16, 2026

Executive Summary

The Problem

Foundation is a transformative 8-week experience — but what happens on Week 9? Without a clear next step, leaders lose momentum, habits fade, and organizations lose their training investment.

The Solution

Ascent turns a one-time training into an ongoing leadership operating system. It gives graduates:

  • Direction — Focused 6-8 week "sprints" targeting specific skills
  • Accountability — Monthly feedback from their actual team members
  • Proof — Visible evidence that their leadership is improving over time
  • Challenge — Progressively harder scenarios that keep growth interesting

Why Leaders Stay Engaged

Ascent leverages four psychological hooks that keep leaders coming back month after month:

Hook What It Does Why It Works
External Validation Team members rate the leader monthly "My team noticed!" is more powerful than any badge
Identity Investment Leaders publicly commit to focus areas Once you say "I'm working on feedback," you work on it
Unlockable Challenges New skills unlock only after proving mastery Gamification done right — earned, not given
Visible Progress Trend lines show improvement over time Seeing the line go up is addictive

User Journey: From Graduation to Ongoing Growth

Moment 1: The Graduation Celebration

When: Leader completes Foundation Session 5 (final milestone)

What They Experience:
  • Celebrates the accomplishment (dopamine hit)
  • Provides personalized insights they can't get elsewhere
  • Creates momentum to continue immediately

Moment 2: Choosing Your Focus (Ascent Onboarding)

When: Immediately after graduation modal

What They Experience:
  • Personalized recommendations feel tailored, not generic
  • Observer commitment creates accountability before Day 1
  • Low friction — they're already in the app, momentum is high

The Three Steps:

  1. Welcome presentation explaining Sprints, Pulses, and Unlocks
  2. Focus selection based on Foundation data
  3. Invite 2-5 observers to provide monthly feedback

Moment 3: The Sprint Dashboard (Daily Experience)

When: Every time the leader opens the app during an active sprint

What They Experience:
  • Clear progress visualization (visual feedback loop)
  • Countdown to observer pulse creates anticipation
  • Specific weekly focus keeps it actionable, not overwhelming

Key Components:

  • Sprint progress (e.g., 7 of 12 reps completed)
  • Next pulse date and observer count
  • This week's specific focus
  • Trend visualization showing confidence improvement

Moment 4: The Observer Pulse (Monthly Feedback)

When: End of each month during active sprints

Why This Is the Killer Engagement Feature:
  • External validation is more powerful than self-assessment
  • Monthly rhythm creates recurring app opens
  • Trend visualization shows real, measurable growth
  • Comments provide specific, actionable feedback
  • The notification "Your team noticed!" triggers immediate curiosity

Observer Experience: No login required, 30-second survey with one question about the leader's focus area

Leader Experience: Sees aggregated results by relationship type, trend compared to self-rating, and anonymous comments

Moment 5: Sprint Completion & Unlocks

When: Leader completes their 6-8 week sprint

Why This Matters:
  • Clear celebration moment (completion feels good)
  • Unlocks create anticipation and investment
  • AI narrative provides personal meaning
  • Immediate prompt to start next sprint maintains momentum

The Unlock System: Leaders unlock new advanced scenarios, modules, and badges based on their performance and progress

Moment 6: The Situation Room (On-Demand Crisis Prep)

When: Leader has a high-stakes moment coming up

Why This Matters:
  • Immediate utility — helps with real problems right now
  • Personalized coaching based on their actual patterns
  • Creates habit of returning to app before hard moments
  • Debrief loop turns real situations into learning

What Leaders Get: Customized prep including opening words, likely reactions and responses, and personalized coaching based on their history

The Engagement Flywheel

The system is designed around a powerful feedback loop:

  • Practice: Leaders log real conversations (Real Reps)
  • Measurement: Visible improvement shown through trend lines
  • Validation: Observer pulse creates external proof of change
  • Motivation: This drives continued practice and engagement
The key insight: Self-reported progress is okay. External validation is addictive.

Competitive Differentiation

  • ✗ Training ends after the workshop
  • ✗ Progress is self-reported
  • ✗ Content is generic
  • ✗ Learning is passive consumption
  • ✗ Certificates prove attendance
  • ✗ Development is annual

Ascent

  • ✓ Training becomes an operating system
  • ✓ Progress is externally validated
  • ✓ Challenges are personalized to gaps
  • ✓ Learning is active practice + feedback
  • ✓ Trend lines prove behavior change
  • ✓ Development is continuous
"Foundation taught you what to practice.
Ascent proves you've changed — to your team, your boss, and yourself."

For Leaders: A system that keeps them growing without requiring willpower.
