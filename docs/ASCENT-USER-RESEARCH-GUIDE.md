# User Research: Foundation Graduate Interviews
## Interview Guide for Ascent Validation

**Created:** March 10, 2026  
**Status:** Ready for interviews  
**Purpose:** Validate Ascent concept with Foundation graduates before building

---

## Research Objectives

### What We Need to Learn

1. **Gap Identification** — What do graduates feel is missing after Foundation?
2. **Habit Status** — Are they still practicing Real Reps? Why or why not?
3. **Feature Value Ranking** — Which Ascent features resonate most?
4. **Observer Appetite** — Would they invite direct reports to give feedback?
5. **Pricing Sensitivity** — What would they pay for continued development?
6. **Retention Signals** — What would bring them back weekly?

### Success Criteria

- Interview 5-8 Foundation graduates
- 60%+ express interest in at least one Ascent pillar
- Identify top 2 features by enthusiasm
- Uncover 2-3 concerns or objections to address

---

## Interview Protocol

### Setup

- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Format:** Video call (Zoom/Teams) or in-person
- **Recording:** Ask permission to record for notes
- **Incentive:** $50 gift card or free month of Ascent

### Introduction Script

> "Thanks for joining me! We're designing the next phase of LeaderReps — what comes after Foundation — and your perspective as a graduate is invaluable.
>
> This is a conversation, not a test. There are no right answers. I want to hear your honest experience — the good and the frustrating.
>
> Everything you share will stay confidential. We'll use patterns across interviews to shape the product, not individual quotes.
>
> Sound good? Any questions before we start?"

---

## Section 1: Foundation Experience (5-7 minutes)

### Opening Questions

**1.1 Overall Experience**
> "How would you describe your overall Foundation experience in a few words?"

*Listen for: Satisfaction levels, specific highlights, frustrations*

**1.2 Biggest Takeaway**
> "What was the single biggest thing you took away from Foundation?"

*Listen for: Skills learned, mindset shifts, specific techniques*

**1.3 Favorite and Least Favorite**
> "What was your favorite part of Foundation? And what did you find least valuable?"

*Listen for: Widget engagement, coaching quality, content relevance*

---

## Section 2: Post-Foundation Reality (7-10 minutes)

### Current State Questions

**2.1 Current Practice**
> "How often are you practicing what you learned in Foundation now? Daily? Weekly? Not at all?"

*Listen for: Frequency, triggers, barriers*

**2.2 What Stuck**
> "Which specific skills or habits from Foundation are you still using today?"

*Listen for: Real Reps mentioned, specific leadership moments, tools used*

**2.3 What Faded**
> "What have you let slip that you wish you hadn't?"

*Listen for: Accountability gaps, motivation loss, specific habits*

**2.4 App Usage**
> "How often do you open the LeaderReps app now? What brings you back (or doesn't)?"

*Listen for: Usage patterns, dormant vs. active, what would change this*

### Probing Questions (if needed)

- "What would it take for you to practice Real Reps every week?"
- "What's the biggest barrier to continuing your leadership development?"
- "If we built nothing new, would you keep using LeaderReps?"

---

## Section 3: Feature Concept Testing (15-20 minutes)

*Present each concept briefly, then gauge reaction. Watch body language and enthusiasm levels.*

### Concept 1: Leadership Sprints

**Description:**
> "Instead of an open-ended 'keep getting better' experience, imagine 6-8 week focused sprints. You'd pick a skill — like Direct Feedback or Delegation — and go deep on it. Daily Real Reps around that theme. Clear start and finish. Completion badge. Then you choose your next sprint."

**Questions:**
- "What's your initial reaction to that?"
- "Would you prefer structured sprints or open-ended practice?"
- "What sprint topic would you want first?"
- "How long feels right — 4 weeks? 6? 8?"

*Rate enthusiasm: 😐 Low | 🙂 Medium | 😃 High | 🔥 Very High*

---

### Concept 2: Observer Pulses (Team Feedback)

**Description:**
> "Imagine being able to invite your direct reports to give you anonymous monthly feedback — just one quick question each month. You'd see aggregate trends like 'Your team says your direct feedback has improved' or 'Opportunity to work on delegation.' Not individual responses, just patterns."

**Questions:**
- "What's your gut reaction to that?"
- "Would you invite your direct reports to be observers?"
- "How many people would you invite?"
- "What would make you hesitant?"
- "Would seeing 'Your team says you're improving' feel rewarding?"

*Rate enthusiasm: 😐 Low | 🙂 Medium | 😃 High | 🔥 Very High*

---

### Concept 3: Advanced Rep Scenarios

**Description:**
> "In Foundation, you practiced baseline scenarios. Imagine unlocking harder scenarios as you progress — like 'De-escalating a Defensive Response' or 'Navigating Peer Conflict.' These would be earned by completing sprints. More complex, more nuanced."

**Questions:**
- "Does the idea of 'unlocking' harder scenarios feel rewarding or annoying?"
- "What advanced scenarios would you want access to?"
- "Would you practice more if you knew harder scenarios were waiting?"

*Rate enthusiasm: 😐 Low | 🙂 Medium | 😃 High | 🔥 Very High*

---

### Concept 4: Situation Room (Crisis Prep)

**Description:**
> "Imagine a 'Situation Room' where you could go when you have a real crisis — like 'I have to fire someone tomorrow' or 'I need to deliver bad news to my team.' On-demand coaching, prep worksheets, real-time scenario practice."

**Questions:**
- "Have you had a leadership crisis moment since Foundation ended?"
- "If you had, would you have opened LeaderReps for help?"
- "What crisis scenarios come to mind that you'd want prep for?"
- "Would you pay extra for on-demand crisis coaching?"

*Rate enthusiasm: 😐 Low | 🙂 Medium | 😃 High | 🔥 Very High*

---

### Concept 5: Guide Status (Mentoring)

**Description:**
> "After you've completed a couple sprints, you could opt in to become a 'Guide' — pairing with a new Foundation cohort member to answer questions and share real-world stories. You'd get Guide badge, recognition, and help shape the next generation of leaders."

**Questions:**
- "Would you want to be a Guide for new leaders?"
- "What would you need from us to feel equipped to mentor someone?"
- "Would Guide status feel like a reward or a burden?"
- "How much time per week would you give to this?"

*Rate enthusiasm: 😐 Low | 🙂 Medium | 😃 High | 🔥 Very High*

## Section 4: Business Model (5 minutes)

### Pricing Questions

**4.1 Value Perception**
> "If Ascent had these features, what would you expect to pay per month? Don't try to guess what we're charging — what would feel fair to you?"

*Listen for: Price anchoring, corporate vs. personal budget perception*

**4.2 Corporate vs. Personal**
> "Would you expense this to your company, or pay out of pocket?"

*Listen for: Budget owner, approval friction, buying power*

**4.3 Annual Commitment**
> "If there was a discount for annual commitment (like $X/month vs. $Y/year), would that appeal to you?"

*Listen for: Commitment willingness, discount sensitivity*

---

## Section 5: Wrap-Up (3-5 minutes)

### Final Questions

**5.1 Must-Have**
> "If you could wave a magic wand, what's the ONE thing Ascent absolutely must have for you to use it weekly?"

**5.2 Deal-Breaker**
> "Is there anything that would make you NOT want to use Ascent?"

**5.3 Anything Else**
> "Is there anything I didn't ask about that you think we should know?"

### Thank You Script

> "This has been incredibly helpful. Your input is directly shaping what we build next.
>
> As a thank you, [mention incentive — gift card, free month, etc.].
>
> Would it be okay if we reach out again in a few months to show you what we've built?"
